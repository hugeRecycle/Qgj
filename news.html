Recent News
  • Oct 17, 2024 Published online:

    Our Paper on “One-Step Diffusion Distillation through Score Implicit Matching” was accepted by NeurIPS 2024, where we introduce the method “score implicit matching (SIM)”, which enables to transform pre-trained multi-step diffusion models into one-step generators in a data-free fashion.

    Related Entities URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2410.16794

  • August 24, 2024 Published online:

    Our Paper on “Equilibrated Diffusion: Frequency-aware Textual Embedding for Equilibrated Image Customization” was accepted by ACM Multimedia 2024 as oral project, where we demonstrate that Equilibrated Diffusion surpasses other competitors with better subject consistency while closely adhering to text descriptions, thus validating the superiority of our approach.

    Published: Equilibrated Diffusion: Frequency-aware Textual Embedding for Equilibrated Image Customization | Proceedings of the 32nd ACM International Conference on Multimedia

  • April 30, 2024 Published online:

    Our Paper on “UltrAvatar: A Realistic Animatable 3D Avatar Diffusion Model with Authenticity Guided Textures” was accepted by CVPR 2024, where we demonstrate the effectiveness and robustness of the proposed method, outperforming the state of-the-art methods by a large margin in the experiments.

    Published: UltrAvatar: A Realistic Animatable 3D Avatar Diffusion Model with Authenticity Guided Textures | IEEE Conference Publication | IEEE Xplore

  • April 30, 2024 Published online:

    Our Paper on “OmniMotionGPT: Animal Motion Generation with Limited Data” was accepted by CPR 2024, where we present OmniMotionGPT, a new framework that trains on sparse animal motion data and generates diverse motions from complex texts by transferring learned human motion knowledge. Additionally, we introduce AnimalML3D, the first text-animal motion dataset with 1240 animation sequences spanning 36 different animal identities.

    Published: OmniMotionGPT: Animal Motion Generation with Limited Data | IEEE Conference Publication | IEEE Xplore
